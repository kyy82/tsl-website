Multi-Armed Bandit Problem:
You face a classic exploration vs exploitation dilemma. Should you pull the arm that seems best so far (exploit), or try other arms to gather more information (explore)? The epsilon-greedy algorithm balances this by choosing the best known arm most of the time, but exploring randomly with probability $\epsilon = 0.1$.

Algorithm:
$$a_t = \begin{cases} \arg\max_a Q_t(a) & \text{with probability } 1-\epsilon \\ \text{random action} & \text{with probability } \epsilon \end{cases}$$ where $Q_t(a) = \frac{1}{N_t(a)} \sum_{i=1}^t r_i \mathbf{1}_{a_i = a}$ is the estimated mean reward.
